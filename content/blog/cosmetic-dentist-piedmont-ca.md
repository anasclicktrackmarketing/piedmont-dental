## What Is Cosmetic Dentistry?

Cosmetic dentistry is the branch of dental care focused primarily on enhancing the appearance of your teeth, gums, and overall smile. While restorative dentistry repairs damage or disease, cosmetic dentistry improves esthetics—often accomplishing both goals simultaneously. Modern procedures are personalized, predictable, and increasingly comfortable, with results lasting a decade or longer with proper maintenance.

## Invisalign Clear Aligners

Invisalign is an FDA-cleared clear aligner system that gradually shifts teeth into optimal alignment using custom-fitted, virtually invisible trays—without brackets, wires, or dietary restrictions of traditional braces.

**Ideal for:**

- Crowding and spacing issues
- Gaps between teeth
- Overbites and underbites
- Mild-to-moderate bite irregularities

**Key advantages:**

- Virtually invisible
- Fully removable for eating and oral hygiene
- Typically more comfortable than traditional braces
- Fewer office appointments
- Results visualized through 3D treatment planning

## Professional Teeth Whitening

Professional teeth whitening is the most affordable and fastest cosmetic treatment available. Office-based whitening far surpasses over-the-counter options, using clinically validated bleaching agents at concentrations unavailable in retail products.

Results typically last 12 to 24 months, depending on diet, oral hygiene, and tobacco use. The practice offers both in-office sessions and customized take-home kits, with materials designed to minimize sensitivity.

## Porcelain Veneers

Ultra-thin porcelain shells bonded to tooth surfaces, veneers correct discoloration, chips, cracks, gaps, uneven edges, and slight misalignment simultaneously, often in two appointments.

Dr. Martenson and Dr. Ma approach veneer design collaboratively, capturing esthetic goals and facial proportions before precise specifications go to a dental ceramics laboratory. With proper care, porcelain veneers typically last 10 to 15 years.

## Dental Bonding

Composite dental bonding offers an elegant and cost-effective solution for minor chips, small gaps, surface stains, or uneven edges. Shade-matched composite resin is applied directly to the tooth surface, sculpted, shaped, and polished—sometimes in a single appointment without anesthesia or laboratory work required.
