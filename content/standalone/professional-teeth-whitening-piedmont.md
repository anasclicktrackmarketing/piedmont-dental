## What Is Teeth Whitening?

Teeth whitening is a cosmetic dental treatment that lightens discolored teeth and removes surface stains. The main ingredients, usually hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide, break down stains into smaller pieces. This makes your teeth look whiter.

## Common Causes of Tooth Discoloration

Understanding why your teeth become stained can help determine the best whitening approach:

- Food & Drinks: Coffee, red wine, soda, and berries cause staining over time.
- Tobacco Use: Smoking and chewing tobacco lead to deep yellow or brown stains.
- Poor Oral Hygiene: Inadequate brushing allows stains and plaque buildup.
- Aging: Enamel wears down, revealing darker dentin beneath.
- Medications: Certain drugs like tetracycline can discolor teeth.
- Fluorosis: Excess fluoride during development can cause white or brown spots.

## Natural Teeth Whitening: Do They Work?

DIY methods like baking soda, charcoal, and oil pulling have gained popularity but lack strong scientific support. Some can be abrasive and damage your enamel. Always consult your dentist before trying natural teeth whitening remedies.

## How Long Do Whitening Results Last?

Results typically last between 6 months and 3 years, depending on your lifestyle and oral care habits. You can prolong the effects by:

- Avoiding staining foods and drinks
- Drinking through a straw
- Practicing good oral hygiene
- Using whitening toothpaste
- Scheduling touch-ups at Piedmont Dental by Design

## Is Teeth Whitening Safe?

Yes, when done under the supervision of a dental professional, teeth whitening is very safe. Side effects are usually minor and may include:

- Tooth Sensitivity – Usually temporary and treatable with sensitivity toothpaste
- Gum Irritation – Mild and short-lived
- Enamel Damage – Only occurs with overuse or misuse of products

## Teeth Whitening FAQs

**Q: Can everyone whiten their teeth?**
A: Most people can. However, whitening does not work on crowns, veneers, or fillings.

**Q: How white can my teeth get?**
A: Results vary, but you can typically lighten your smile by several shades.

**Q: What about deep or intrinsic stains?**
A: Whitening is less effective on internal stains caused by trauma or medication. In these cases, we may recommend veneers or cosmetic bonding.
