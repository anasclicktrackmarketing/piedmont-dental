## CEREC Crowns for Quick Results

When a tooth becomes severely damaged or decayed, a filling cannot prevent the tooth from having further issues. A crown is usually the best option for covering and protecting the tooth. Our dentist is committed to offering innovative and modern restorative dental options whenever you visit. This is why we provide CEREC one-day crowns for your convenience.

CEREC crowns are made from a strong ceramic using our computer-aided design (CAD) and manufacturing (CAM) system. We take detailed digital images of your tooth and jaw before designing your crown. Your crown is made in our office and to our specifications for an aesthetically pleasing and comfortable solution. Typically, CEREC crowns are made the same day as your visit to keep from interrupting your schedule with further dental appointments.

## Expert Piedmont Dental Implants

When you only have a single tooth missing, an implant-supported crown may be the solution you are looking for. An implant-supported crown also prevents further bone or tissue loss that is common when teeth are missing.

## Dental Restoration FAQ's

**Q1: What is the best option for replacing a missing tooth?**

A1: Dental implants are often considered the best option for replacing a missing tooth due to their durability and natural appearance. However, your dentist will recommend the most suitable option based on your specific needs.

**Q2: How long do dental restorations last?**

A2: The longevity of dental restorations varies depending on the type of treatment and your oral hygiene habits. Generally, dental implants can last 20 years or more, while crowns and bridges may last 10–15 years.

**Q3: Are dental restoration procedures painful?**

A3: Most dental restoration procedures are performed under local anesthesia to ensure comfort. Any post-procedure discomfort can typically be managed with over-the-counter pain relievers.

**Q4: How do I care for my dental restorations?**

A4: Maintaining good oral hygiene, including regular brushing and flossing, is essential. Additionally, regular dental check-ups will help ensure the longevity of your restorations.

**Q5: Will my dental insurance cover restoration treatments?**

A5: Coverage varies depending on your insurance plan. Our team can assist you in understanding your benefits and exploring financing options if needed.
