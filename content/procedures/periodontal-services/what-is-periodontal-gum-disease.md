# Understanding Periodontal Disease: Protect Your Health

## What is a periodontist?

Periodontists are qualified dentists who have an additional three years of education and specialized training in the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of gum disease.

## What are some warning signs?

Gum disease is described as a "silent" disease because pain doesn't always accompany warning signs. Patients should see a dentist if experiencing:

### Changes to your Teeth, Bite or Dental Work

- Loose or separating teeth
- Changes in bite alignment
- Bridges/partial dentures that no longer fit properly
- Defective fillings

### Changes to Your Gums

- Recurring redness, puffiness, tenderness, or swelling
- Bleeding while brushing, flossing, or eating hard foods
- Gums pulling away (receding) from teeth

### Bad Breath or Odd Taste in Your Mouth

- Persistent bad breath
- Persistent metal taste

### Sores in Your Mouth

- Pus between gums and teeth
- Sores/irritations not improving within two weeks

## How do we treat gum disease?

Treatment depends on infection stage and deterioration extent. After reviewing digital X-rays and performing a thorough exam, the periodontist discusses options, including procedure details, office visits required, post-treatment healing care, and long-term disease management.

### Non-Surgical Treatment

The most well-known type of non-surgical treatment is known as scaling and root planing. This under-the-gum procedure removes plaque and tartar from tooth roots, eliminating harmful bacteria and irritants.

### Surgical Treatment

#### Pocket Reduction

Local anesthetic applied, gum tissue folded back, damaged bone smoothed, bacteria removed, tissue closed and sutured.

#### BONE REGENERATION

Local anesthetic applied, gum tissue folded back, bacteria removed, membranes/bone grafts/tissue-stimulating proteins used to encourage natural regeneration and reverse damage.
