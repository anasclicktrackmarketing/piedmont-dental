## What is Periodontics?

Periodontics is a specialty branch of dentistry that focuses on protecting and treating gum health while also caring for your teeth and jawbone. Periodontal treatments are designed to address gums tissue concerns to ensure your teeth are supported by a healthy and strong foundation for many years to come.

The core aspects of periodontal care includes:

**Prevention** – Keeping your gums healthy through a combination of regular care, education, and proactive treatment.

**Treatment** – Procedures designed to address gum disease at every stage and eliminate the infection.

**Maintenance** – Protecting your gums after recovery and preventing re-infection.

## How Gum Disease Affects Oral Health

Some medications and specific health conditions, such as pregnancy-related hormone changes and diabetes, can increase gum disease. Gum infection can also cause complications with cardiovascular health, arthritis, and Alzheimer's.

Gum disease is an oral infection that attacks the gums, and in severe cases, can deteriorate the jawbone and teeth. The condition can potentially enter your bloodstream and cause infections throughout the body if left to progress.

## Treating Gum Disease in Piedmont

Your treatment depends on your stage of gum disease and the level of infection. While it is possible to reverse the condition in its earliest stages, the infection is not reversible in more advanced cases. Our care focuses on repairing and restoring your gums and doing everything we can to prevent future re-infection.

Our non-surgical treatments include:

**Professional Cleanings** – Bi-annual, routine cleanings are essential for keeping your gums healthy and free of disease. Our hygienists remove dental plaque, tartar, and food debris from teeth and hard to reach areas. This treatment can help prevent further gum inflammation.

**Tooth Scaling & Root Planing** – When gum disease is present, our skilled hygienists perform deep cleanings to stop and treat the infection. ## Surgical Periodontal Treatments

Many of our surgical procedures are designed to treat gum disease in advanced stages. Our periodontist can also perform treatments to help renew the look and feel of your gums.

Our surgical procedures include:

### Bone Grafts

Bone grafts are necessary when advanced gum disease or prolonged tooth loss has left your jawbone weak. This procedure adds new bone material into your jaw to restore its health and density. We also offer bone grafts to prepare for dental implant placement.

### Gum Grafts

This regenerative procedure is designed to correct gum tissue recession. Receding gums typically result from advanced gum disease. Our periodontist can take healthy tissue from the roof of your mouth and graft it to the areas of your gums in need of treatment.

### Sinus Lifts

When there is not enough room between your sinuses and upper jaw, or the area is too weak, a sinus lift may be necessary. Our specialist will often add new bone in the area of your upper jaw to make it taller. Sinus lifts may also be necessary to prepare for dental implant placement.

### Crown Lengthening

Our periodontist can perform crown lengthening for many reasons. We can reshape your gums to prepare for a dental crown, restore aesthetics after treating gum disease, or gain access to deep decay.

## Understanding Periodontal Disease — Early Signs and Risks

Periodontal disease is a chronic infection that affects the gums and the bone supporting teeth. It often begins as **gingivitis** and can progress into **periodontitis** without timely treatment.

**Common signs of periodontal disease include:**

- Gum redness or swelling
- Bleeding when brushing or flossing
- Persistent bad breath
- Gum recession
- Tooth sensitivity or mobility

If left untreated, periodontal disease can lead to tooth loss and is linked to serious systemic conditions such as heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and respiratory problems.

## Periodontal FAQ's

**Q1: How can I tell if I have gum disease?**

A1: Signs include bleeding gums, swelling, bad breath, gum recession, and tooth sensitivity. A dentist can diagnose periodontal disease during routine exams.

**Q2: What is the best treatment for gum disease?**

A2: It depends on the stage. Early gingivitis can be treated with professional cleaning and improved home care. Advanced periodontitis may require scaling, root planing, gum grafts, or bone grafting.

**Q3: How often should I schedule periodontal maintenance?**

A3: Most patients benefit from periodontal cleanings every 3–4 months after treatment to prevent recurrence.

**Q4: Can periodontal disease be prevented?**

A4: Yes. Proper oral hygiene, regular dental visits, and professional cleanings can significantly reduce the risk of periodontal disease.

**Q5: Does gum disease affect overall health?**

A5: Yes. Gum disease is linked to heart disease, diabetes complications, stroke, and other health conditions. Treating gum disease supports overall wellness.
